<p><p>The waterborne polyurethane resin market for synthetic leather is primarily divided into two types: dry process and wet process. The dry process involves applying a solvent-free polyurethane coating, resulting in less environmental impact and improved durability. In contrast, the wet process uses water as a solvent, allowing for greater flexibility and ease in application but may require careful management of drying times. Both methods aim to enhance the quality and performance of synthetic leather products, catering to diverse consumer preferences.</p></p>
